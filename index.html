The two senses

One sensor can be fooled. Two, rarely.

Infrared alone flags every warm radiator and sunbeam. Acoustic alone flags every footstep and passing truck. LeakWatch only raises an alert when both signatures agree, in the same place, at the same time.

Infrared thermography

A microbolometer array scans the pipe jacket for the faint cold (or hot, on heating loops) plume that escaping water leaves behind, down to a 0.05°C difference invisible to the hand.

  • Reads surface temperature, not just air temperature
  • Works through insulation cladding and behind drywall
  • Maps the plume's shape to estimate flow direction

Acoustic emission sensing

Piezoelectric transducers clamp to the pipe and listen in the 20–80kHz band, where pressurised water forced through a crack produces a persistent hiss that normal plumbing noise doesn't.

  • Detects leaks as small as 0.1 litres per minute
  • Locates the leak along the pipe run by triangulating arrival time
  • Filters out flow noise from valves, pumps and taps in use

Deployment sequence

From site visit to standing alert, in four passes

  1. 01

    Pipe run survey

    We walk the accessible pipe runs with a handheld thermal camera to flag insulation gaps, prior repairs and likely failure points before any sensor is fixed in place.

  2. 02

    Sensor placement

    Acoustic transducers are clamped at intervals set by pipe material and diameter; infrared units are mounted with a clear sightline to the longest exposed runs and known joints.

  3. 03

    Baseline calibration

    The system records seven days of normal acoustic and thermal activity, so it learns what your building's pumps, traffic and sun exposure look like before flagging anything as unusual.

  4. 04

    Live monitoring

    Once both channels agree on a deviation, the system locates it along the run and sends a location, a confidence score and a snapshot of both readings to your maintenance queue.
